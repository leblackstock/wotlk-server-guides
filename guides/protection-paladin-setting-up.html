WotLK 3.3.5 Setting up your Tankadin

Choose the talents, glyphs, macros, and addons that support reliable Protection Paladin play.

◆ Talents

Recommended shape: Use a standard 0/53/18-style raid build, then move the small number of flexible points based on which raid debuffs are already covered. The exact tree matters less than keeping every core survival talent and the correct 969 timing.
Fully filled baseline

0/53/18 balanced raid build

Use this complete tree as the starting build. The few flex points described below can move when the raid already supplies the same debuffs or utility.

Tree not loading? Open the complete build in the Wowhead calculator.

Core survival talents

  • Improved Righteous Fury for threat and damage reduction.
  • Toughness, Anticipation, and Sacred Duty.
  • Holy Shield and its supporting block talents.
  • Ardent Defender for low-health mitigation and cheat death.
  • Combat Expertise for stamina and expertise.
  • Touched by the Light for spell scaling from Strength.
  • Guarded by the Light to refresh Divine Plea.
  • Shield of the Templar and Hammer of the Righteous.

Raid utility talents

  • Judgements of the Just 2/2 if nobody else reliably provides the 20% attack-speed slow.
  • Vindication 2/2 if the raid lacks a full attack-power reduction.
  • Divine Sacrifice + Divine Guardian for a powerful raid damage-reduction cooldown.
  • Blessing of Sanctuary for damage reduction and mana return while tanking.
  • Spiritual Attunement needs only enough points to keep mana stable in your content.

Threat and flex points

  • Improved Judgements 1/2 is the clean 969 choice. Two points shorten it to 8 seconds and can cause collisions.
  • Deflection, Conviction, and Crusade are the usual Retribution-side threat path.
  • Pursuit of Justice is excellent movement utility if you are not relying on Tuskarr's Vitality.
  • Reckoning is mostly a fun or niche threat talent, not a progression priority.
  • Divinity is flexible, but do not strip stronger mitigation or raid utility to force five points into it.

Recommended major sets

General raid: Divine Plea + Seal of Vengeance + Righteous Defense.

Threat set: Divine Plea + Seal of Vengeance + Judgement.

Planned cooldown set: Divine Plea + Seal of Vengeance + Salvation.

Trash set: Divine Plea + Hammer of the Righteous + Righteous Defense or Seal.

Minor glyphs

Glyph of Sense Undead: free 1% damage against Undead while tracking them. Very useful in Wrath.

Glyph of Lay on Hands: lowers the cooldown of a major emergency button.

Glyph of Blessing of Kings: practical third minor for cheaper rebuffs.

◆ Macros: what to use, and when not to

Spell-name warning: These use the exact names from an English 3.3.5 client. Horde uses Seal of Corruption; Alliance must replace it with Seal of Vengeance. Test every macro somewhere harmless before trusting it in a raid.
Macro rule: Use macros to reduce targeting mistakes and bar clutter. Do not use them to surrender encounter decisions. A macro cannot see the add behind you, the tank-swap timer, or the healer currently inventing new swear words.

Optional two-button 969 helper

Training wheels, not autopilot: The two-button helper can teach the 9 → 6 rhythm while you learn, but it cannot choose the right spell for the moment. reset=9 and reset=6 reset only after that many seconds without pressing the macro; they do not watch cooldowns or reset automatically every 9 or 6 seconds. A failed cast, silence, movement, target swap, missed button, early Holy Shield refresh, or deliberate Consecration skip can desynchronize the sequence. Keep the individual abilities visible and be ready to take over.

9-slot button

Cycles Holy Shield, your assigned Judgement, and Consecration.

Use whenYou are learning the alternating rhythm in forgiving content and keep the individual spells visible.
Do not use whenHoly Shield must be refreshed early, Consecration should be skipped for mana or movement, or your Judgement assignment changes.
#showtooltip
/castsequence reset=9 Holy Shield, Judgement of Wisdom, Consecration

6-second button

Alternates Hammer of the Righteous and Shield of Righteousness.

Use whenYou want a simple rhythm helper while learning and can immediately recover with the individual abilities.
Do not use whenYou need a specific strike now, are target-swapping, or one ability failed to cast. The sequence advances only on successful casts.
#showtooltip
/castsequence reset=6 Hammer of the Righteous, Shield of Righteousness
Why the full one-button rotation macro is not recommended

The screenshots and video demonstrate fixed castsequence ideas, but a complete one-button 969 chain is brittle. It cannot react to movement, silences, add spawns, target swaps, missed pulls, an early Holy Shield refresh, or a deliberate Consecration skip. It is useful only as a training experiment in harmless content. The two-button version above is easier to diagnose and recover when reality kicks the rotation table.

Tank-control and emergency macros

Smart taunt

Taunts from a friendly mouseover or friendly target with Righteous Defense; uses Hand of Reckoning on a hostile target.

Use whenYou need one button for friendly rescue-taunts and direct enemy taunts, especially in dungeons or add-heavy fights.
Do not use whenYou cannot clearly see what your mouse is over. Righteous Defense may taunt multiple mobs attacking that friendly player.
#showtooltip
/cast [@mouseover,help,nodead] Righteous Defense; [help,nodead] Righteous Defense; [harm,nodead] Hand of Reckoning

Pull: Hand of Reckoning + Avenger's Shield

Attempts the taunt first, then throws Avenger's Shield for a hard ranged opener.

Use whenYou are intentionally starting a pull and want immediate snap threat on an unowned target.
Do not use whenAnother tank owns the mob, crowd control is nearby, or you need Avenger's Shield saved for a caster or runner.
#showtooltip Avenger's Shield
/cast Hand of Reckoning
/cast Avenger's Shield

Bubble / cancel bubble

First press casts Divine Shield. A later press removes it so you can resume tanking.

Use whenA planned tank swap lets you clear a dangerous debuff, or an immunity is required and the other tank is ready.
Do not use whenYou are the only active tank. Immune targets are skipped, so the boss can immediately hit someone else.
#showtooltip Divine Shield
/cancelaura Divine Shield
/cast Divine Shield

Divine Sacrifice cancel

Activates Divine Sacrifice, then removes the transfer aura while retaining Divine Guardian's raid reduction on standard 3.3.5 behavior.

Use whenYour build includes Divine Guardian and the raid needs a planned damage-reduction cooldown.
Do not use whenYou have not tested our server's scripting. Some cores process the aura or cancellation differently.
#showtooltip Divine Sacrifice
/cast Divine Sacrifice
/cancelaura Divine Sacrifice

Self Salvation defensive

For Glyph of Salvation: casts Hand of Salvation on yourself for its damage reduction.

Use whenYou have a large threat lead, during a tank swap, or for a planned burst window where threat loss is acceptable.
Do not use whenYou are establishing threat, DPS is close behind, or the glyph is not equipped.
#showtooltip Hand of Salvation
/cast [@player] Hand of Salvation

Sacred Shield mouseover

Places Sacred Shield on a friendly mouseover; otherwise it defaults to you.

Use whenYou normally self-cast but occasionally need to protect an off-tank or another assigned target without changing targets.
Do not use whenYour mouse is resting over the wrong raid frame, or another paladin's stronger/assigned Sacred Shield should remain untouched.
#showtooltip Sacred Shield
/cast [@mouseover,help,nodead][@player] Sacred Shield

Bar-saving modifier and setup macros

Judgement modifier

Default: Light. Shift: Wisdom. Alt: Justice. This matches the modifier concept shown in the video.

Use whenYour judgement assignment changes between groups and you can reliably remember the modifiers.
Do not use whenAlt or Shift is already used for self-cast, focus-cast, paging, or another binding. Accidental Justice is not a raid strategy.
#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift] Judgement of Wisdom; [mod:alt] Judgement of Justice; Judgement of Light

Seal modifier

Default: boss seal. Shift: Command for cleave. Alt: Wisdom for mana recovery.

Use whenYou swap seals often and want the choices on one predictable key.
Do not use whenYou are maintaining five Corruption/Vengeance stacks or modifiers are easy to press accidentally during movement.
#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift] Seal of Command; [mod:alt] Seal of Wisdom; Seal of Corruption

Three-press self-buff setup

Cycles Righteous Fury, Blessing of Sanctuary, and the boss seal after a death or full rebuff.

Use whenYou have just resurrected, changed specs, or lost your self-buffs and want a compact recovery button.
Do not use whenYou are already buffed, PallyPower assignments require a different blessing, or you are mid-pull. Each successful step uses a global cooldown.
#showtooltip
/castsequence [@player] reset=10 Righteous Fury, Blessing of Sanctuary, Seal of Corruption

◆ Addons

Use the 3.3.5 version: The links below point to Wrath-compatible releases. Install one addon at a time, enable “Load out of date AddOns” only when necessary, and test it on a target dummy or easy dungeon before trusting it in a raid.

Paladin-specific addons

PallyPower

What it is for: Assigning Greater Blessings across classes, checking who is missing a Paladin buff, and quickly maintaining your own aura, seal, blessing, and Righteous Fury. In a raid, it lets Paladins coordinate assignments instead of negotiating blessings in chat.

What it is not for: It does not choose the raid's best blessing plan for you, play your character, or replace communication. Coordinated assignments work best when the other Paladins also run PallyPower, and assignments normally cannot be changed during combat.

Paladin utilityStrong recommendation

Protection Is Surprisingly Stupendous

What it is for: A Protection Paladin 9696 training aid that shows which configured ability is available next. It can help a new tank recognize the alternating 9-second and 6-second rhythm.

What it is not for: It does not cast spells, handle taunts, track threat, warn about bosses, or know when encounter mechanics require you to break the normal rotation. It is a discontinued 2009 beta, so treat it as optional and expect that some 3.3.5 clients or private-server builds may produce errors.

General tank and raid addons

Deadly Boss Mods

What it is for: Boss ability timers, prominent warnings, sounds, and encounter-specific alerts. It helps you anticipate tank swaps, movement, dangerous casts, and other scheduled mechanics.

What it is not for: It is not a threat meter, rotation helper, or substitute for learning the fight. Timers can drift when our server uses custom scripting or different encounter timing, so react to the boss and combat log rather than obeying a bar blindly.

Essential raid toolServer timing may vary

Omen3

What it is for: Showing your group's relative threat on the current enemy so you can see whether another player is approaching or passing the tank. It is especially useful during pulls, threat resets, and tank swaps.

What it is not for: It does not guarantee who a boss will attack, detect every taunt failure, or explain encounter-specific aggro rules. Immunities, fixates, threat wipes, range thresholds, and private-server combat-log behavior can all make the real target choice differ from a simple threat bar.

Tank essentialThreat visibility

OmniCC

What it is for: Adding readable numeric countdowns directly to action buttons, items, and abilities while they are on cooldown. It makes the 969 rhythm and defensive cooldown availability much easier to read at a glance.

What it is not for: It does not recommend the next spell, track whether Holy Shield or Divine Plea is active, or warn about boss mechanics. It only turns the normal cooldown sweep into numbers.

Simple and usefulCooldown numbers

WeakAuras

What it is for: Building custom icons, bars, sounds, and warnings for buffs, debuffs, cooldowns, health, mana, and combat events. For Protection, good first trackers are Holy Shield, Divine Plea, Sacred Shield, Righteous Fury, Forbearance, taunt cooldowns, and major defensives.

What it is not for: It is not a ready-made Protection setup by itself and it does not make decisions for you. A blank installation does very little until you create or import auras, and imported packages should be reviewed rather than trusted blindly.

Highly customizableSetup required

RatingBuster

What it is for: Expanding item tooltips with rating conversions and easier comparisons, making old-client stats such as Defense, dodge, parry, hit, and expertise less cryptic while evaluating upgrades.

What it is not for: It is not a BiS list, simulation, or encounter-aware gearing adviser. It does not know whether you already have 540 Defense, whether a socket bonus is worthwhile, or whether survival matters more than threat for the fight in front of you.
