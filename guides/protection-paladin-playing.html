◆ Pull playbook

Choose the card that matches the pull. Click any card to open it alone in a focused view. Keep the listed buffs active, prepare before contact, use the correct taunt, and follow the short opening into your normal rhythm.

Routine dungeon pull

Melee trash pack

Enemies that run into melee and remain grouped.

Trash

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Seal of Command Devotion / encounter aura

Before contact

Divine Plea› Sacred Shield› Holy Shield

Pull

Avenger's Shield into the pack, meet it at the stack point, and place Consecration where the mobs will remain.

Correct taunt

One loose enemy: Hand of Reckoning. Several attacking one ally: Righteous Defense on that ally. A clean planned pull normally needs neither.

Opening rhythm

Avenger's Shield› Consecration› Hammer› Judgement› Shield

Continue 9 → 6 and tab-target when threat is uneven.

If it goes wrong

  • One enemy peels: Hand of Reckoning, then tab back after threat is secure.
  • Several reach an ally: Righteous Defense on that player, then Hammer of the Righteous.
  • The pack spreads: regroup at the tanking point and place the next Consecration where it ends up.
Ranged or mixed trash

Caster / spread pack

Enemies that cast from range instead of stacking naturally.

Trash

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Seal of Command Devotion / resistance aura

Before contact

Divine Plea› Sacred Shield› Holy Shield

Choose a safe line-of-sight corner or stack point before starting.

Pull

Avenger's Shield the key caster, then use line of sight or cover to bring the ranged enemies into your stack point.

Correct taunt

One caster on someone else: Hand of Reckoning. Several attacking one ally: Righteous Defense. Taunting does not force a caster to move while it can still cast; use line of sight.

Opening rhythm

Avenger's Shield› Move / line of sight› Consecration› Hammer

Build the melee cluster first, then resume 9 → 6 while controlling the outliers.

If it goes wrong

  • A caster will not move: break line of sight or stun it when possible; do not chase into another pack.
  • Several target the healer: Righteous Defense on the healer, then establish threat at the stack point.
  • The silence fails: control the melee cluster first and use Hand of Reckoning or the next Avenger's Shield on the outlier.
Recovery and new spawns

Loose mob / add pickup

A new enemy appears or an existing enemy leaves the tank.

Pickup

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Encounter seal Encounter aura

Before contact

Keep Divine Plea, Sacred Shield, and Holy Shield maintained. Know the spawn point and save a pickup tool when adds are expected.

Pull

Taunt the correct target, meet it with a high-threat strike, then move it into the established tanking area.

Correct taunt

One hostile target: Hand of Reckoning directly. Up to three enemies attacking one friendly target: Righteous Defense on that player.

Pickup rhythm

Correct taunt› Hammer› Best 9-slot› Shield

Resume 969 without placing the two 6-second strikes back-to-back.

If it goes wrong

  • The taunt misses or is immune: announce it and use Avenger's Shield, Shield, or Hammer to build threat until you can retry.
  • Several adds split: Righteous Defense on the endangered player, then Hammer and Consecration.
  • Your rhythm breaks: place the best available 9-slot between the two 6-second strikes.
Single-target encounter

Standard boss pull

A boss that can be met and held near its starting position.

Boss

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Corruption / Vengeance Devotion / encounter aura

Before contact

Divine Plea› Sacred Shield› Holy Shield

Use a planned potion. Use Avenging Wrath only when its protection-spell lockout is safe.

Pull

Hand of Reckoning as the boss becomes attackable, meet it, and turn it away from the group. Pre-place Consecration only if it will remain there.

Correct taunt

Hand of Reckoning directly on the boss. Use Righteous Defense only when rescuing a friendly target already being attacked.

Opening rhythm

Reckoning› Judgement› Hammer› Consecration› Shield

Continue 9 → 6 while maintaining Holy Shield and five seal stacks.

If it goes wrong

  • DPS pulls threat: Hand of Reckoning, follow with a high-threat strike, and ask for a threat drop.
  • The boss faces the group: fix its facing before chasing the next rotation button.
  • Your health dives: use Divine Protection and a health consumable, then call the emergency clearly.
Positioning pull

Moving boss pull

A boss that must be dragged away from its starting position.

Boss

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Corruption / Vengeance Encounter aura

Before contact

Divine Plea› Sacred Shield› Holy Shield

Choose the destination and path. Do not pre-place Consecration at the starting point.

Pull

Hand of Reckoning, then back or strafe along the planned route. Establish position and facing before planting Consecration.

Correct taunt

Hand of Reckoning directly on the boss. Righteous Defense is a rescue option if the boss has already attached to an ally.

Opening rhythm

Reckoning› Judgement› Hammer› Holy Shield› Shield

Use Consecration only after the boss reaches the intended tanking position.

If it goes wrong

  • The boss stops short: re-establish control at its hitbox; do not leave Consecration behind the boss.
  • The boss faces the group: correct position and facing before resuming the rotation.
  • Movement breaks 969: place the best available 9-slot between the two 6-second strikes.
Coordinated raid handoff

Tank swap

Taking a boss from another tank on an assigned cue.

Raid

Keep active

Righteous Fury Sanctuary Corruption / Vengeance Encounter aura

Before contact

Refresh Divine Plea and Sacred Shield, move into the assigned position, and activate Holy Shield immediately before the swap.

Pull

On the swap call, Hand of Reckoning the boss, confirm that it turns to you, then settle its facing and position.

Correct taunt

Hand of Reckoning is the safe boss-only swap. Righteous Defense can taunt additional enemies attacking the co-tank, so avoid it when that tank is holding adds.

Opening rhythm

Reckoning› Judgement› Shield› Holy Shield› Hammer

Confirm the swap succeeded before committing attention to the rotation.

If it goes wrong

  • The taunt fails or is immune: call it immediately so the previous tank keeps the boss and uses a cooldown if needed.
  • The co-tank also has adds: do not use Righteous Defense; target the boss with Hand of Reckoning.
  • You receive the boss at low health: use Divine Protection or a health consumable and stabilize before chasing threat.

◆ The 969 rotation

Pattern: 9 → 6 → 9 → 6. Cast one ability from the 9-second group, then one ability from the 6-second group, and keep alternating. Holy Shield and Consecration have 8-second base cooldowns, but each occupies a 9-second slot once global cooldowns are woven between casts. Use exactly 1/2 Improved Judgements to reduce Judgement from a 10-second cooldown to 9 seconds so it fits that group cleanly.

9-slot: Holy Shield 9-slot: Judgement 9-slot: Consecration 6-sec: Hammer of the Righteous 6-sec: Shield of Righteousness

Clean 969 sequence

9sHoly Shield› 6sHammer of the Righteous› 9sJudgement› 6sShield of Righteousness› 9sConsecration› 6sHammer of the Righteous› 9sHoly Shield› 6sShield of Righteousness

Continue alternating whichever 9-slot ability is due with whichever 6-second strike is due. Maintaining Holy Shield and correct positioning outranks blindly finishing a memorized sequence.

Recovering 969 after movement, a missed cast, or an add pickup
  • Do not rebuild the rhythm by casting Hammer of the Righteous and Shield of Righteousness back-to-back.
  • Place the best available 9-slot ability between the two 6-second strikes.
  • Sacred Shield or Avenger's Shield can fill a gap when using them is actually useful.
  • Holy Shield, positioning, and encounter jobs outrank a perfect sequence.
Rotation macros are training wheels. A one-button castsequence cannot react to movement, silences, target swaps, missed pulls, add spawns, or the need to refresh Holy Shield early. Learn the ability groups even if you keep a helper macro. Open the macro guide.

◆ Cooldowns, seals, and utility

Divine Protection

Your main wall: 50% damage reduction for 12 seconds. Plan it for predictable tank-busters rather than waiting until your health is already a pink smear.

Primary defensiveCauses Forbearance

Ardent Defender

Passive damage reduction below 35% health plus a cheat-death effect. It is a safety net, not permission to ignore mechanics.

PassiveCore talent

Divine Sacrifice / Divine Guardian

Powerful raid damage reduction. The transfer portion can kill you; the common cancel-aura macro removes the transfer while preserving the raid-wall portion on standard 3.3.5 scripting.

Raid cooldownTest on our server

Lay on Hands

Large emergency heal. Know how our server handles its mana effect and Forbearance interactions before using it beside Divine Protection.

Emergency heal

Glyph of Salvation cooldown

Hand of Salvation on yourself gives 20% damage reduction with the glyph while also reducing threat. Best during tank swaps, transitions, or when you have a huge lead. Review the glyph choices.

Threat lossSituational glyph

Divine Shield

Use a bubble-cancel to clear specific debuffs only when the encounter allows it. Divine Shield does not erase your stored threat, but enemies stop treating you as a valid attack target while immune.

Tank-swap riskDebuff clear

Sacred Shield

Maintain it on yourself for a recurring absorb. It lasts 30 seconds and one Paladin can keep it on only one target at a time.

Routine mitigationRefresh before it expires

Avenging Wrath

A snap-threat tool, not a defensive. It prevents Divine Protection, Divine Shield, and Hand of Protection for 30 seconds, so delay it when an early emergency button may be needed.

Threat burst30-second protection lockout

Seals

  • Seal of Corruption / Vengeance: default single-target boss seal.
  • Seal of Command: packs, cleave, and fast-moving trash.
  • Seal of Wisdom: niche mana recovery when incoming healing and Sanctuary returns are low.

Judgements, blessings, and aura

  • Judgement of Wisdom: common default when mana support is useful.
  • Judgement of Light: raid healing support when another Paladin does not provide it.
  • Blessing of Sanctuary: your normal self-buff while tanking.
  • Devotion Aura: default physical mitigation; swap to a resistance aura when encounter damage demands it.
Tier 10 four-piece note

The Protection four-piece bonus turns Divine Plea activation into an additional short defensive window. If our server scripts the set correctly, treat Divine Plea as a planned cooldown as well as a mana tool; verify the exact dodge amount and duration on your character tooltip.
